Behavioral Interview Preparation

Key Stories & Narratives

Story 1: Leading Technical Initiative

A compelling narrative about taking ownership of a complex technical project, overcoming challenges, and delivering measurable impact. Demonstrates leadership, problem-solving, and communication skills.

Story 2: Handling Failure & Learning

An honest reflection on a significant setback or failure, the lessons learned, and how it shaped professional growth. Shows resilience and self-awareness.

Story 3: Cross-Functional Collaboration

Evidence of working effectively with designers, product managers, and other engineers. Demonstrates soft skills and ability to work in a team environment.

Story 4: Innovation & Initiative

An example of proposing and implementing a new solution, tool, or process improvement that benefited the team or product.

STAR Method Framework

All behavioral stories follow the STAR method:

SITUATION: Set the context and background TASK: Describe the challenge or goal ACTION: Explain the specific steps taken RESULT: Quantify the outcomes and impact

Communication & Soft Skills

Interview Communication Strategy

  • Clarity First: Use simple, direct language. Avoid jargon unless appropriate and ensure understanding.
  • Structure Your Answers: Organize thoughts before speaking. Use transitions like "Let me break this down" or "Here are three key points."
  • Ask Clarifying Questions: When unclear, ask for clarification rather than making assumptions.
  • Think Out Loud: In technical interviews, narrate your thought process so interviewers can follow your reasoning.
  • Quantify Results: Use specific metrics and data when discussing accomplishments.
  • Show Enthusiasm: Demonstrate genuine interest in the role, company, and technical challenges.

Important Questions to Ask

  • What does success look like in this role over the next 90 days? 6 months?
  • What are the biggest technical challenges your team is currently facing?

Interview Logistics & Preparation Checklist

Pre-Interview Checklist

1 Week Before
Review company information, products, and recent news. Study main technical topics.
3 Days Before
Practice coding problems and system design. Record yourself answering behavioral questions.
1 Day Before
Get adequate sleep. Review your resume and key talking points. Prepare questions to ask.
30 Minutes Before
Test technology (camera, mic, internet). Find a quiet space. Have water nearby. Take deep breaths.

Day-Of Preparation

  • Dress professionally (or appropriately for the company culture)
  • Use a professional background or appropriate Zoom backdrop
  • Have a copy of your resume visible
  • Keep notes with key stories and questions nearby
  • Eliminate distractions (notifications, other tabs)
  • Test your internet connection
  • Arrive 5-10 minutes early to the meeting

Post-Interview Actions

  • Send a thank-you email within 24 hours
  • Reference something specific from the conversation
  • Express continued interest in the role
  • Ask about next steps and timeline
  • Document questions you were asked for future preparation

Strategic Recommendations

  1. Lead with Confidence: Remember that G2i has already vetted your qualifications. Use the interview to demonstrate fit and enthusiasm.
  2. Show Genuine Interest: Ask thoughtful questions about the company, team, and role. This distinguishes strong candidates.
  3. Balance Technical & Soft Skills: Don't over-emphasize one dimension. Show you're both technically skilled and culturally aligned.
  4. Be Authentic: The best interview outcomes come from genuine connection, not performing a version of yourself.
  5. Follow Up Properly: A thoughtful follow-up email can reinforce your interest and professionalism.

Final Thoughts

Interview success is about demonstrating not just what you know, but who you are and how you work. You've prepared the technical foundation—now focus on authentic communication and genuine interest in the opportunity.

Remember: Interviewers want to hire someone who can do the job AND be great to work with. Both are equally important.
